- Inaccurate wage statements
- Waiting time penalties (failure to pay all wages at termination)

When a commercial property loan is in distress (default or near-default), it gets transferred from the regular loan servicer to a "special servicer" whose job is to:
- Maximize recovery for lenders
- Modify loan terms OR

## DSCR Theory
**DSCR = Net Operating Income / Debt Service**

If ICI is trying to maintain minimum DSCR to avoid further default:
- **Cutting maintenance expenses** to preserve NOI
- **Not responding to complaints** (saves time/money)
- **Giving rent concessions instead of repairs** (keeps rent flowing vs. losing tenants)
- **Short-staffing properties** (per Glassdoor reviews)
- **Not paying vendors/contractors** (per BBB complaints)

The 50% rent concession makes sense:
- Better to collect 50% than lose 100% if tenant leaves
- New tenant would demand repairs before signing
- Repairs = capital expense = kills NOI = DSCR drops = deeper default
